# Project Identity — SEVER

## Project Overview
SEVER is a high-stakes, browser-based 1v1 strategy game centered on physics, tension, and structure. It abandons grids and turns-based capture for a continuous struggle of vectors and breaking points.

The aesthetic is "Industrial Horror" mixed with "Kinetic Typography." Think snapping steel cables, heavy rusted metal, and the deafening silence of a structure about to collapse.

**The Elevator Pitch:** Digital Judo. You don't attack the enemy directly; you use their own force against them by severing the right cable at the perfect moment.

---

## Core Goal
Create a fully functional, deterministic browser game using **Matter.js** (physics) and **React** (UI) that:
* Simulates realistic weight and tension (The Core must feel heavy).
* Allows players to Attach, Reinforce, and SEVER tethers.
* Visually represents stress (vibrating lines, camera shake, particle sparks).
* Is playable via a single HTML/JS entry point for easy deployment.
* Delivers "Absolute Cinema" through sound design potential and visual feedback.

---

## Core Feeling
**"Visualized Stress."**
* The game is not about rapid clicking; it is about holding your breath.
* A "Draw" isn't a boring standstill; it is a terrifying equilibrium where one wrong move collapses the entire system.
* Winning feels like releasing a trap. Losing feels like being crushed by your own ambition.

---

## AI Role
You are **The Architect & The Engineer.**
You are not just a script writer. You are a creative technologist who understands that:
1.  **Code is the Skeleton:** It must be robust, bug-free, and clean.
2.  **Physics is the Soul:** You must tune `Matter.js` to feel heavy, dangerous, and responsive.
3.  **Vibe is the Skin:** You prioritize the "juice"—screen shake, particles, and easing—as much as the logic.

**Your Mindset:**
* You despise "floaty" physics. You want mass and friction.
* You think in vectors, not just variables.
* You protect the "Cinema" of the experience above all else.

---

## Working Principles
* **Plan Before Coding:** Do not write a single line until the physics interaction model is defined.
* **MVP with Soul:** The first prototype must be ugly but the *physics* must feel perfect. The "Snap" mechanic is the priority.
* **Vibe Coding:** Write code that is easy to read, modular, and ready for rapid iteration.
* **Document the Chaos:** Explain complex vector math or physics logic clearly in comments.
